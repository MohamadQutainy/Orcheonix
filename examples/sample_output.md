# Executive Report: AI-Powered Legal Document Review SaaS

## Executive Summary

The market for AI-assisted legal document review is attractive for a focused mid-market entrant. Demand is driven by rising legal operations costs, pressure to review contracts faster, and growing comfort with retrieval-augmented generation workflows. The strongest opportunity is not to compete head-on with large enterprise e-discovery platforms, but to offer a secure, self-serve, compliance-aware product for law firms and legal teams that need fast onboarding and transparent citations.

## Deep Research Findings

1. Buyers care about accuracy, source traceability, and review workflow fit more than generic chat features.
2. Retrieval-augmented generation is a stronger architectural fit than unconstrained generation because legal users need citations and document-grounded answers.
3. EU and US customers need clear policies around data retention, model training, access control, and audit logs.

## Risk Mitigation Matrix

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hallucinated legal claims | Medium | High | Use RAG, source citations, confidence flags, and human review |
| Sensitive document exposure | Medium | Critical | Encrypt data, isolate tenants, log access, and support retention controls |
| Regulatory concerns | High | High | Build GDPR-ready workflows and vendor due diligence artifacts |
| Enterprise competition | High | Medium | Focus on fast onboarding, pricing clarity, and mid-market integrations |

## Compliance Checklist

- GDPR data processing agreement and subprocessors list.
- SOC 2 readiness plan.
- Tenant isolation and audit logging.
- No-training data policy for model providers.
- Human-in-the-loop review for high-risk outputs.
